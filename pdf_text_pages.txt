“Service relationship A cooperation between a service provider and service consumer. 
Service relationships include service provision, service consumption, and service 
relationship management." 
“Service provision: Activities performed by an organization to provide services:
•
Management of the provider´s resources, configured to deliver the service
•
Ensuring access to these resources for users
•
Fulfilment of the agreed service actions
•
Service level management and continual improvement
Service provision may also include the supplying of goods." 
“Service relationship management: Joint activities performed by a service provider and a 
service consumer to ensure continual value co-creation based on agreed and available 
service offerings.” 
„Service consumption: Activities performed by an organization to consume services:
•
Management of consumer´s resources needed to use the service
•
Service actions performed by users, including utilizing the provider´s resources, 
and requesting service actions to be fulfilled
Service consumption may also include the receiving ( acquiring ) of goods “.
source : ITIL Foundation ITIL 4 Edition (2019)

Problem

Problem

Error Control
1.
Problem Identification Phase: 1) Trend analysis of incident records, 2) Input 
from users, service desk, technical support teams, 3) Identified risks from major 
incident Process , 4) Supplier/Partner Input, 5) Input by SW developers, test 
teams and project teams
2.
Problem control phase: Analysis of problems and documentation of 
workarounds and known errors: 
High-priority problems are analyzed (technical + processes + information) in 
order to identify causes and solve them. If problems cannot be solved in the 
foreseeable future, workarounds to resolve the incident are documented . These 
can also be retained in the long term if problem solutions are too expensive or 
not feasible.
3.
Error control phase: The aim is to identify problems that have already been 
analyzed and errors localized, for which a permanent solution is to be developed 
and implemented. Known errors are regularly re-evaluated (customer impact, 
cost and availability of the solution, quality of the workaround )
